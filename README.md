# Customer-Segmentation
Buisness Analytics Project
1. Executive SummaryThis project aims to analyze customer purchasing behavior to help a retail business design targeted marketing strategies. By using the K-Means Clustering algorithm, we grouped 200 customers into five distinct segments based on their Annual Income and Spending Score. These insights allow the business to move away from "one-size-fits-all" marketing and instead focus on high-value targets.2. Problem StatementMany businesses struggle to understand which customers contribute most to their revenue. Traditional marketing often wastes budget on customers who are unlikely to buy. This project solves that by identifying specific groups (segments) so the company can:Increase Customer Lifetime Value (CLV).Reduce marketing waste.Improve customer satisfaction through personalized offers.3. Data DescriptionThe dataset used is the Mall Customers Dataset, which includes:CustomerID: Unique ID for each customer.Gender: Male or Female.Age: Customer age.Annual Income (k$): Yearly earnings.Spending Score (1-100): A score assigned by the mall based on customer behavior and spending nature.4. Methodology: K-Means ClusteringWe utilized the K-Means algorithm, a machine learning technique that groups data points that are similar to each other.We determined the optimal number of groups (5) using the "Elbow Method."The algorithm calculates the distance between data points to ensure that customers in the same group have similar spending habits.5. Results & Business InsightsBased on the analysis, we identified 5 Key Segments:SegmentLabelIncomeSpendingMarketing Strategy0SensibleLowLowFocus on basic necessities and discounts.1StandardAverageAverageSend newsletters and general promotions.2Target/VIPHighHighPrimary Target. Offer loyalty rewards and luxury previews.3CarelessLowHighTarget with "Flash Sales" and trendy items.4SkepticsHighLowSend personalized premium offers to encourage spending.6. ConclusionBy implementing this segmentation strategy, the business can optimize its marketing budget. Focusing on Segment 2 (High Income, High Spending) ensures the highest return on investment, while tailored strategies for other groups help in building long-term brand loyalty.
